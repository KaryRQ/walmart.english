Platform Improvement Pickers

Rol: UX Research
Role: UX Research
Methods: Interviews with platform users (internal and external pickers), user personas, user journeys, benchmarking, prioritization with the Iceberg model

Objective

The goal of this project was to optimize the interaction between pickers, clients, and stores, aiming to increase productivity and ensure that pickers could perform their work more efficiently. .

Interviews

We engaged with both internal and external pickers to gain a deeper understanding of their daily work. This information allowed us to explore how to merge two existing applications into a single platform, ensuring it remained functional and adaptable to the different delivery formats.

User personas

From the interviews, we identified key differences between internal and external pickers. Based on this, we created detailed user personas that outlined each group’s needs and challenges. This helped us better understand their contexts and informed the design of their user journeys.

User journeys

With the user personas defined, we developed user journeys for each profile. These visualizations helped us identify shared touchpoints, potential interactions between the two profiles, and opportunities to improve their experience in a unified platform. At the same time, we highlighted the most critical pain points that needed to be addressed.

Benchmarking

A market analysis was conducted to review competitors and understand how their delivery platforms for pickers were structured. Both national and international examples were evaluated, allowing us to identify best practices and propose solutions that would be more functional and effective for both picker profiles.

Prioritization with Iceberg Model

Using the insights gathered throughout the research, we created a prioritization matrix based on the Iceberg model. This matrix highlighted the most critical pain points to both product and business teams, justifying the urgency of addressing each one. As a result, business stakeholders were able to make decisions that had an immediate impact on pickers’ experience without requiring deep structural changes to the platform at that stage.
